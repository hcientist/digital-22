Radia Perlman

"Sometimes called the 'mother of the Internet,' a title she rejects, Radia Perlman is an American computer programmer and network engineer born in 1951. As one of the most influential women in computer science, she still works in the field as of 2022.

Her important contributions to computer science include spanning tree protocol, a technology that changed the way networks communicate with each other. Perlman also wrote two computer science textbooks and taught at the University of Washington and Harvard University. She has received more than 80 patents throughout her career. (source: Most Influential Women in Computer Science)
